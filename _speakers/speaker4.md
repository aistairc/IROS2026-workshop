Prof. Kensuke Harada (Fellow, IEEE) received the B.Sc., M.Sc., and Ph.D. degrees in mechanical engineering from Kyoto University, Kyoto, Japan, in 1992, 1994, and 1997, respectively. From 1997 to 2002, he was a Research Associate with Hiroshima University, Hiroshima, Japan. Since 2002, he has been with the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST). From 2005 to 2006, he was a Visiting Scholar with the Department of Computer Science, Stanford University, Stanford, CA, USA, and the Leader of the Manipulation Research Group, AIST, from 2013 to 2015. He is currently a Professor with the Graduate School of Engineering Science, The University of Osaka, Toyonaka, Japan. His research interests include mechanics and control of robot manipulators and robot hands, biped locomotion, and motion planning of robotic systems.
